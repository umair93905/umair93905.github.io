After completing my first semester, I started my second semester, which introduced me to more advanced subjects. One of the main subjects was Object-Oriented Programming (OOP), which was a completely new experience compared to Programming Fundamentals.

In OOP, I learned about important concepts such as objects, classes, and attributes. These concepts helped me understand how real-world problems can be represented and solved through programming. Initially, it was difficult to understand how everything worked together, but with practice, things became clearer.

We also learned about driver classes and templates, which helped us organize and structure our code properly. These concepts improved my ability to write clean and efficient programs.

The lab sessions were very helpful because they allowed me to apply what I learned in theory. Through continuous practice, I improved my coding skills and developed better logical thinking.

OOP taught me how to break down complex problems into smaller parts and solve them step by step. This subject played an important role in building my programming foundation and preparing me for future technical challenges.

Overall, learning OOP was a valuable experience that enhanced my confidence and skills as a Computer Engineering student.
