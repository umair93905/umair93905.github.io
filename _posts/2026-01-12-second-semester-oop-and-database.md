Along with OOP, another important subject in my second semester was Database Management. This subject introduced me to the world of data handling and management, which is a key part of modern software systems.

In Database, we started by learning about different types of keys, such as primary keys and foreign keys, and their importance in maintaining data integrity. After that, we learned how to create tables and perform different operations like `SELECT`, `INSERT`, and `DELETE`.

One of the most interesting parts was learning how to join tables, which allowed us to work with data from multiple sources. This helped me understand how real-world databases function.

We also learned how to create complete databases and manage them effectively. In addition, we started learning how to upload our work on GitHub, which is an essential skill for developers. This gave me exposure to version control and project management.

At the same time, OOP concepts continued to strengthen my programming skills. Combining OOP with Database knowledge gave me a better understanding of how software applications are built and managed.

Overall, this semester helped me develop both programming and data management skills, which are essential for my future career.
