My name is Muhammad Umair, and I am currently a second-semester student of Computer Engineering at UET Faisalabad. I belong to Jura Sakesar, District and Tehsil Sargodha. My academic journey reflects a gradual transformation, where I explored different fields before finding my interest in technology and engineering.

I completed my matriculation from Dar-e-Arqam Model High School Bhalwal with 871 marks out of 1100 in Biology. At that stage, my academic direction was inclined toward medical sciences. However, as I progressed further, I started thinking more deeply about my future and career goals. I realized that my interests were shifting toward technology and problem solving.

Keeping this in mind, I made an important decision at the college level to switch my field from Biology to Computer Science. I enrolled in Punjab College Bhalwal in ICS (Physics). This decision played a crucial role in shaping my future. During my intermediate studies, I worked hard and achieved 899 marks out of 1200. This gave me confidence to pursue a technical field in higher education.

After completing my intermediate education, I entered a phase of uncertainty where I had to decide my career path. Initially, I did not have a clear plan, but I was determined to explore opportunities. Over time, my interest in engineering and computing grew stronger, which eventually led me to pursue Computer Engineering.

Currently, I am studying at UET Faisalabad and have completed my first semester with a GPA of 2.94. Although it is not perfect, it reflects my transition from college to university and the challenges I faced along the way. I am continuously working on improving my academic performance and technical skills.

Overall, my journey has taught me the importance of adaptability, hard work, and self-belief. I am motivated to keep learning and growing in the field of Computer Engineering.
